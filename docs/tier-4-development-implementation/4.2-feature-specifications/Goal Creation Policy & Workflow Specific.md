# Goal Creation Policy & Workflow Specification

## 1. Purpose
Defines roles, responsibilities, and processes for goal creation, assignment, updates, and tracking within the Goal Management System to ensure clarity, accountability, and alignment across the organization.

## 2. Goal Creation — Roles & Responsibilities

### 2.1 Company-level Goals
- Creator: Executive Leadership (CEO, CTO, COO, etc.)  
- Approver: Board or Executive Committee (if applicable)  
- Timing: Quarterly or annually, aligned with strategic planning cycles  
- Process:
    1. Leadership drafts company objectives during strategic planning.
    2. Objectives are reviewed and finalized with key stakeholders.
    3. Once approved, objectives are entered into the Goal Management System.
    4. Key Results are defined by leadership with input from department heads.

### 2.2 Department-level Goals
- Creator: Department Head / VP  
- Approver: Relevant Executive Sponsor  
- Timing: Within 1–2 weeks after company goal finalization  
- Process:
    1. Department heads review company goals and identify relevant objectives.
    2. Department goals are crafted to directly support company Key Results.
    3. Goals are reviewed with the executive sponsor for alignment and approval.
    4. Approved goals are cascaded to relevant teams.

### 2.3 Team-level Goals
- Creator: Team Lead / Manager  
- Approver: Department Head  
- Timing: Within 1 week of department goal finalization  
- Process:
    1. Team leads review department goals and define team objectives.
    2. Team goals should map clearly to department Key Results.
    3. Goals are reviewed with the department head for alignment.
    4. Approved goals are assigned to team members or sub-teams.

### 2.4 Individual-level Goals
- Creator: Employee (with manager guidance)  
- Approver: Direct Manager  
- Timing: Within 1–2 weeks of team goal finalization  
- Process:
    1. Employees draft personal goals aligned with team objectives.
    2. Goals may include performance, development, or project-based outcomes.
    3. Manager reviews and approves goals during 1:1 meetings.
    4. Goals are finalized and set to “Active” in the system.

## 3. Goal Assignment & Ownership

### 3.1 Ownership Rules
- Each goal must have one primary owner.
- Owner responsibilities:
    - Update progress regularly
    - Adjust confidence scores
    - Add evidence or comments
    - Notify stakeholders of risks
- Contributors may be added to support the goal but do not share primary accountability.

### 3.2 Assignment Workflow
1. Goal is created by the appropriate role (see Section 2).
2. Owner is assigned at creation (defaults to creator if not specified).
3. If owner is not the creator, the system notifies the owner via email/in-app notification.
4. Owner acknowledges acceptance of the goal (optional confirmation step).
5. Goal appears in owner’s “My Goals” dashboard.

## 4. Goal Update & Tracking Process

### 4.1 Update Frequency
- Company/Department Goals: Bi-weekly or monthly check-ins  
- Team Goals: Weekly updates recommended  
- Individual Goals: Updated as progress occurs (at least bi-weekly)

### 4.2 Update Responsibilities
- Goal Owner: Primary responsibility for updating progress and confidence.  
- Manager: Reviews updates, provides feedback, and may adjust confidence or status.  
- Contributors: May suggest updates or provide evidence; owner maintains control.

### 4.3 Tracking & Review Cycles
- Weekly: Team leads review team goal progress in team meetings.  
- Bi-weekly: Managers review individual goals in 1:1s.  
- Monthly: Department heads review department goal alignment.  
- Quarterly: Executive review of company goals and overall progress.

## 5. Accountability & Governance

### 5.1 Escalation Path
1. If a goal is At Risk, the owner notifies their manager.
2. Manager assesses and may escalate to the department head.
3. Department head may adjust resources, timelines, or goal scope.
4. Critical risks are flagged in the executive dashboard.

### 5.2 Change Management
- Goal titles, targets, and deadlines may be changed by the owner with manager approval.
- Goal ownership may be reassigned by the current owner’s manager.
- Archived goals require approval from the goal’s original approver.

### 5.3 Compliance & Transparency
- All goal changes are logged in the audit trail.
- Previous versions of goals are retained for reference.
- Goal visibility settings enforce privacy while maintaining necessary transparency across management chains.

## 6. Integration with Performance Management
- Goals are visible in performance review cycles as part of achievement tracking.
- Completed goals contribute to recognition and reward systems.
- Goal confidence and status influence talent and succession planning.

## 7. Success Metrics for Goal Governance
- ≥ 90% of goals have a clearly identified owner  
- ≥ 80% of goals updated within the last 14 days  
- ≤ 10% of goals in “At Risk” status at any time  
- ≥ 95% of cascaded goals align with parent goals
